Fundamentals of Quantum Computing

Overview

This week we will see an overview of the foundations of quantum information theory and quantum computing. To do this, we will follow closely learning resources provided by IBM Quantum. In particular, we will complete the "Basics of Quantum Information" course, and explore some material from the "Fundamentals of Quantum Algorithms" course.

More specifically, we will do the following:

  • Lecture 1: Single systems - classical and quantum.
  • Lecture 2: Multiple systems - classical and quantum.
  • Lecture 3: Quantum circuits
  • Lecture 4: Entanglement in action
  • Lecture 5: Quantum algorithmic foundations

Assignments

1. Your primary assignment is to earn the badge for the "Basics of Quantum Information" course, by passing the exam at the end of the course. To access the exam and receive the badge, you will need to register for an IBMid - ask me if you have a problem with this!

2. In addition to earning the badge, you are required to submit a written explanation for every one of your answers in the exam.

3. If you click on the links for each lecture above, you will see that each lecture comes with a variety of code examples in Qiskit which illustrate the concepts in the lecture. You are also required to submit a Jupyter notebook for each lecture, showing that you have succesfully run all the code examples. Take your time, play around with the examples, and make sure you understand them - because fluency in Qiskit is going to be important in Week 2!

Bonus. You will receive bonus points if by the end of the three week course you have also earned the badge for the "Fundamentals of Quantum Algorithms" course.
